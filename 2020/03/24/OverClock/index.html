OverClock

How much does overclock improve the performance of my PC?

I’m currently studying and gaming on an X570 platform which performances quite well. However, I don’t wanna waste the extra money I spent on this ROG motherboard instead of a MSI B450, I’m going to press this Ryzen 7 3700X more.

I’ve tested the performance of my desktop using 3DMark before trying to overclock it, and this is what I’ve got.

It’s a nice result though, I wanna see if she can reach 10000. I increased the multiplier from 36.00 to 40.50 while the bus speed remained 100MHz. The Wraith Prism is a high-quality one for a cooler sold with the boxed CPU, but I don’t think that it is capable for extreme overclock.

The memories are two 8GB Trident Z which are designed to work at 3200MHz, BUT I didn’t realize that unless I change the BIOS settings their frequency would maintain 2133MHz only. I’d try 3200MHz first with a low timing sequence(14 14 34 75) and see if they could work stably.

Results have been improved a little but the score is still lower than 10,000. What’s also increased is the core temperature. I’m afraid that she won’t pass the stress test if I keep increasing the multilier. Anyway, I’ll have a try.

If the goose hangs high the CPU frequency should be 4200MHz now…. but the reality is I couldn’t reboot her unless I reduce it to 4100MHz. Meanwhile the timing sequence of memories went so high when I push the frequency to 3800MHz that little improvement would be made, I suppose.
